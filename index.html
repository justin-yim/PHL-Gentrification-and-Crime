Philadelphia: Relationship between Falling Crime and Gentrification

Gentrification refers to the influx of middle-class or affluent people into deteriorating areas, which often displaces poorer residents. A recent study suggested that looking for areas for falling crime maybe an effective way to predict gentrification.

This map visualisation aims to apply the theory in Philadelphia, trying to see if there is a relationship falling crime and soaring home sales prices.


Limitation: As the crime data available online were collected by police district/ service area boundaries, they could not be shown by zip code boundaries for clearer comparison with the change in home sale prices. Besides, there are other variables to examine the extent of gentrification, including rent level and demograhpic change. They were not shown in this visualisation due to time constraint.

Data Source: Open Data Philly and The Pew Charitable Trusts
